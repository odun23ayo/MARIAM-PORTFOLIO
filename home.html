Types of Diabetes

Diabetes is defined as an elevated blood sugar, but there are many causes of an elevated blood sugar. An estimated 25.8 millions Americas have diabetes, with another 79 million considered to have "pre-diabetes." Diabetes is classified into different types, based on its cause.

Type 1 Type 2 Gestational
Prevalence Most common form of diabetes in children (also called "juvenile diabetes"). Accounts for about 5% of adult Diabetes cases. Most common form of diabetes , representing 90 to 95% of diabetes worldwide. About 3 to 8% of pregnant women in the U.S. develop this condition .
Cause An autoimmune disorder, in which the body's immune system mistakenly destroys its insulin-producing cells, making the person unable to produce the insulin that delivers sugar into their cells. A dual defect of resistance to the action of insulin, combined with an inability to make enough insulin to overcome the resistance. The hormones of pregnancy or a shortage of insulin.

source: UCSF Diabetes center and National Institutes of Health
